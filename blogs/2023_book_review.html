This year’s reading was rather diverse, from romantic fantasy fiction to self-help books. Here are my top picks of 2023:

Favorite author: Erik Larson
Erik Larson was an easy choice for favorite author of the year as I read 3 of his books. Larson is a true historian. When you read his books you become immersed in the time period and context. Reading his books is the easiest way to learn about history by accident.

The Splendid and the Vile gives insight into the life of Winston Churchill. Churchill lived a fascinating life. Larson dives deep into the personal life of Churchill. The reader can quickly come to the conclusion that Churchill was one of the strangest men to ever live, but he was the right man for the job.

Reading doesn’t get old. In the midst of life and all the responsibilities that come with it, reading stays the same. I’m able to enter into a safe space where I can escape from this reality and enter another. I only say this to emphasize that there is great joy in reading. In a time where electronic screens rule, reading is a true solace for the mind and a place to rest and center oneself.
